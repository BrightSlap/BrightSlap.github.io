1. LEO - Low Earth Orbit

The Low Earth Orbit is where most satellites orbit around the Earth multiple times per day. These are useful for capturing images of the surface. This is also where the International Space Station orbits Earth.

2. SSO - Semi-Synchronous Orbit

The Semi-Synchronous Orbit is where Global Positioning System Satellites are located, they orbit the Earth once every 12 hours.

3. GEO - Geosynchronous Orbit

The Geosynchronous Orbit is the orbital period of approximately 24 hours. Objects in this orbit remain in a fixed location when observed from Earth's surface.

4. Interplanetary Explorations

Many satellites have been launched across the Solar System to study the Planets and their Moons that reside in it. Most notable explorations have been with the Rovers on Mars.
